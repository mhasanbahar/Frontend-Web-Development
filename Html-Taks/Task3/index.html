Necessary materials

For Mantı Dough:

  • 3.5 cups of flour
  • 1 cup of lukewarm water
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 egg

For Mantı Filling:

  • 250 g lean minced meat
  • 1 medium-sized onion
  • 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon of salt

To Cook Mantı:

  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 2 tablespoons of tomato paste

For Mantı Sauce:

  • Garlic Yogurt
  • 1 teaspoon of mint
  • 1 teaspoon of red pepper flakes
  • 1/2 teaspoon of sumac.

Manti Making Stage

  1. In a deep mixing bowl, add 3.5 cups of flour and 1 teaspoon of salt. Mix and create a well in the center of the flour.
  2. Add 1 egg into the center and continue mixing. Slowly incorporate 1 cup of lukewarm water while stirring.
  3. Knead the dough until it reaches a consistency that is neither too firm nor too soft. Cover the ready dough with plastic wrap and let it rest.
  4. While the dough is resting, in another suitable mixing bowl, add 250 grams of lean minced meat. Then, add 1 medium-sized onion, 1/2 teaspoon of black pepper, 1 teaspoon of salt, and 1/2 teaspoon of red pepper flakes. Mix the ingredients thoroughly.
  5. Place the dough on a floured surface and shape it by kneading. Divide the dough into three equal parts.
  6. Turn the divided dough into small balls. On the floured surface, roll out each ball with a rolling pin to form thick sheets.
  7. Cut the rolled-out dough into small squares using a knife. Place small portions of the prepared minced meat mixture in the center of each square.
  8. Fold the four corners of the dough to the center, giving it a dumpling shape. Place the shaped mantı on a floured tray. Freeze the tray to solidify the mantı.
  9. After removing the frozen mantı from the freezer, transfer them to plastic bags with double locks and store them in the freezer for later use. Boil water in a suitable pot.
  10. Cook the frozen mantı in boiling water for about 15-20 minutes. In a saucepan, melt 2 tablespoons of butter, then add 2 tablespoons of tomato paste and mix well.
  11. Pour the prepared tomato paste sauce into the cooked mantı and continue cooking while stirring. Place the cooked mantı on a deep plate and pour garlic or regular yogurt over them. Your mantı recipe is ready. You can serve it with added red pepper flakes, mint, and sumac.
